Overview

Nocturne Journal is a private dream journal. This policy explains exactly what data the app handles, where it is sent, what is stored, and what is not. It is written to match the app's actual behaviour rather than to read well in marketing.

Voice Recordings — You Choose How They Are Transcribed

When you record a dream, Nocturne always transcribes the audio first on your device using Moonshine, a local speech recognition model. After that, the app's behaviour depends on a setting you control:

  • Cloud transcription (the default). Once recording finishes, the audio is sent to OpenAI's Whisper transcription service to produce a more accurate transcript. OpenAI's audio transcription endpoint operates under a Zero Data Retention agreement: audio is processed in real time, is not stored on OpenAI's servers, and is not used to train their models. The audio leaves your device only for the seconds it takes to be transcribed.
  • On-device only. You can switch this off in Settings → Cloud transcription. With cloud transcription disabled, the on-device Moonshine transcript is kept and your audio never leaves your phone.

Either way, the audio file itself is kept only in your app's private storage. It is never uploaded to our servers and is never included in cloud sync.

Dream Text and Journal Data

Your dream text, titles, tags, emotions, themes, and journal entries are stored locally on your device in a private SQLite database. They are not shared with anyone unless you explicitly trigger a feature that requires it (see the next two sections).

If you create an account and turn on cloud sync, the text portion of your journal is also stored in Supabase under your user account. Audio files and the device-side paths to them are always excluded from sync.

AI Artwork, Analysis, and Enhancement

When you ask Nocturne to generate artwork, analyse a dream, or enhance a description, the relevant dream text is sent to OpenAI to produce the result. OpenAI's API terms state that API content is not used to train their models. The text is processed to return a response and is not retained for training purposes.

Face Swap (Optional)

If you use the face swap feature, the reference photo you provide and the dream artwork are sent to Replicate's API for processing. The composited image is returned to your device. Neither the reference photo nor the source image is stored on our servers, and Replicate's API processes them transiently for the single request.

Image Sharing

The app includes an optional "Share Artwork" feature. When you share:

  • Only the artwork image is shared — never your dream text, transcription, audio, emotions, or personal details.
  • A confirmation prompt appears before any share action proceeds.
  • Sharing is always user-initiated.

Account and Email

Creating an account is optional. If you sign up, your email address is stored in Supabase for authentication only. It is not sold, not shared with third parties, and not used for marketing without your explicit consent.

Device Identifier

To enforce free-trial limits and prevent abuse of the AI generation features, Nocturne sends a one-way hashed device identifier (derived from Android's standard ANDROID_ID) to our backend. We do not see the raw identifier, we do not link it to your name or email, and it is used only to count generations against your free allowance. If you create an account, your user id is used in place of the device id.

Analytics and Tracking

Nocturne does not use analytics SDKs, advertising identifiers, or tracking pixels. We do not track your behaviour across other apps or websites. The app includes Sentry for crash reporting in production builds only — Sentry receives technical error data (stack traces, app version, device model), not the contents of your dreams.

Encryption

All data sent between the app, our backend, and third-party services is encrypted in transit (TLS/HTTPS). Cloud-stored data in Supabase is encrypted at rest in Supabase's infrastructure. Your on-device SQLite journal is stored in your app's private storage area but is not encrypted at rest on the device; consider Android's device encryption (on by default for recent Android versions) your primary protection there.

Data Retention and Deletion

You can delete an individual dream at any time from the dream detail screen. You can clear all local data from Settings → Journal Data → Delete All Data, or by uninstalling the app. If you signed up for an account and want your cloud data deleted, including after uninstalling, email nocturnerecorder@threeeyedemu.com.au and we will action the request and confirm in writing. Account deletion removes your row in Supabase Auth and all dream rows linked to your user id.

Third-Party Services

  • OpenAI — Speech-to-text transcription (Whisper, Zero Data Retention) when cloud transcription is enabled; text processing for AI artwork, analysis, and prompt enhancement. OpenAI Privacy Policy
  • Replicate — Image processing for the optional face swap feature. Replicate Privacy Policy
  • Supabase — Optional account authentication, optional cloud sync of dream text, and storage of generated artwork images. Supabase Privacy Policy
  • Sentry — Crash reporting in production builds only. Sentry Privacy Policy

Children

Nocturne is not directed at children under 13 and is rated for adult users. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us so we can take appropriate action.

Changes to This Policy

We will update this policy if our data practices change. The current version is always accessible at this URL and from within the app. Material changes will be highlighted in the app's release notes.
